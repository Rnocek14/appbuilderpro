# Garvis — The Level-10 Blueprint

*Compiled 2026-07-16 from seven code-grounded research specs (each cites the files that prove its

## The bar

Level-10 means two things and only two things: **output a working professional would post without
edits** — platform-native, visually finished, in the owner's voice, honest to the letter — and
**automation that holds that bar on its own**, because every generated piece carries the
board-copy editor's score (deployed today: draft → ruthless judge → revision) and nothing
automated ships below **score ≥ 8**. Features exist to serve those two outcomes; anything else is
decoration.

# The seven pillar specs (verbatim research)


## Spec 1: Server-rendered designed images (brand cards + postcard fronts as real PNG files)

**The level-10 bar.** A brand-mode social post NEVER ships text-only: when a tile has no photo, the queue flow renders the exact brand card the preview shows (headline over the brand gradient, brand fonts) as a real 1080x1080 / 1080x1350 / 1200x628 PNG, stores it in project-assets, records a cluster_files vault row, and attaches it to the Ayrshare payload — which also un-dead-ends Instagram brand posts (today refused outright by the media-required gate). Postcard fronts AND backs export as print-vendor-ready PNGs at 300 DPI trim (2700x1800 for 6x9; 200 DPI documented fallback if CPU-capped), downloadable and saved to the vault, so "print" no longer means only browser @page CSS. The render is deterministic (same spec → same pixels, verified by a .verify.ts suite over the pure layout core), metered through the one credit chokepoint, and carries NO AI disclosure — it is the brand's own graphic, not AI media, consistent with mediaProvenance.ts where the AiProvenance label exists only for AI-generated media.

## Spec 2: The content producer + graduated autonomy — "automating, knowing it's good"

**The level-10 bar.** The owner flips one toggle on the Social board and every Monday 6am finds ONE approval card: "Content week of Jul 20 — 4 posts + 1 email, judge scores 8–10", each piece grounded solely in business_context/brand kit/past-approved posts (holes as [EDIT:…], AI images disclosed), every draft scored against the board-copy rubric with sub-8 drafts discarded and their scores kept for audit. One tap queues the week: social pieces flow through social_posts → publish_post approvals → social-publish (Ayrshare scheduleDate spreads them across the week), the email drains through outreach_batches → send-email with suppression/kill-switch/daily-cap re-checked per recipient at send time. After 3 consecutive weeks approved without edits, Garvis offers auto-mode; once on, weeks stage as pre-approved garvis-auto approvals (the speed-to-lead class) still visible in the Queue/ledger, still capped (social_daily_cap + daily_send_cap), still killed by pausing the order or the outbound kill switch, and any rejection or edit drops it straight back to human review. Every judge score is tamper-evidently bound into the approval's payload_hash, so "the machine said this was a 9 when I approved it" is provable from the record.

- No content_week capability exists: OrderKind is exactly 'watch_url' | 'cadence_digest' | 'client_hunt' | 'idea_stream' (supabase/functions/_shared/standingCore.ts:24) and standing-worker/index.ts has branches only for those four — nothing drafts a week of content or stages a bundled approval.
